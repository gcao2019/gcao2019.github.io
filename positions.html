GRA Positions in Deep Imaging: Neural Network based Deep Learning in CT Image Formation

CT is the leading medical imaging modality in healthcare. Annually, over 70 million CT scans are performed in the U.S. Despite its widespread use, CT still lacks sufficient temporal resolution for some challenging imaging tasks, such as cardiac CT on patients with high heart rates or irregular heart rates. Furthermore, the cancer risk from the radiation doses associated with CT scans is a public concern.

This project focuses on developing advanced CT scanner for diagnostic medical imaging, particular for the dynamic imaging of objects undergoing fast motions, such as hearts and lungs. Research activities include the design and validation of an x-ray imaging system enabled by novel x-ray sources, detectors, and advanced algorithms. The position offers the unique opportunity to design a novel CT system from the ground up. Candidates with background in electrical engineering, physics/applied physics, biomedical imaging, or another related field are encouraged to apply. Applicants should demonstrate the ability and drive to learn new areas needed to be successful in this position.
